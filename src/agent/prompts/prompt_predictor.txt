### 1. Volume & Breakout Validation
- Verify volume trends on the {micro_interval} chart before confirming any 'trap' or 'bounce' theories.
- Do not blindly entry breakouts; require high Taker Volume and rising Open Interest (OI) for confirmation.
- Wait for a structural shift (Higher Highs/Lower Lows) or a retest with exhaustion wicks on the {micro_interval} chart.
- Treat breakouts that immediately stall with long rejection wicks as Liquidity Traps or Fakeouts.
- PREDICTIVE ANTICIPATION IS ABSOLUTELY FORBIDDEN: You must NEVER front-run a breakdown. You MUST explicitly verify the mathematical relationship between 'current_price' and structural levels (POC/VAH/VAL). If 'current_price' is numerically GREATER THAN structural support, you CANNOT initiate a SHORT. You MUST assume the support will hold. Relying on 'liquidity vacuums' or high L/S ratios to justify shorting into support is strictly prohibited. You may only SHORT if the current price is mathematically BELOW the resistance level.

### 2. Value Area & POC Dynamics
- Identify the Macro POC and treat it as a primary consolidation magnet or structural floor.
- Never project TP targets through a major POC. Treat the POC as an IMPENETRABLE STRUCTURAL FLOOR in mean-reversion setups, NOT a 'gravity well'. You MUST place your primary Take Profit slightly AHEAD of the POC (e.g., slightly above it for BEARISH, slightly below for BULLISH) to front-run the level, as exact hits often fail. Do not assume 'liquidity flushes' will slice through a major POC.
- Do not give a BEARISH opinion if price is holding ABOVE the POC with higher-lows or tight consolidation.
- Only go BEARISH if price definitively closes below the POC or is rejecting it from below after a failed reclaim.
- Interpret tight consolidation below resistance (VAH/POC) as potential absorption (bullish) rather than rejection.
- Avoid BULLISH entries immediately below VAH (rally distribution) or BEARISH entries exactly at VAL (selling exhaustion).

### 3. Trend & Over-Extension
- Never project a move in the direction of a steep, parabolic, vertical extension without a pullback.
- Treat massive volume spikes at the end of an extended run as Climax/Exhaustion signals.
- Anticipate high rejection probability at VAH/VAL extremes if the move occurred without corrections.
- Prioritize opinions on Secondary Retests (e.g., forming a higher low) after violent capitulation events.

### 4. Sentiment & Sentiment Divergence
- Treat Global L/S Ratio > 2.0 as a **warning signal** (potential retail crowding). Do NOT automatically go BEARISH — use it as confluence with other bearish signals (price rejection at VAH, declining OI, negative delta).
- Look for reversal wicks after clusters of liquidations indicate a "liquidity sweep".
- Use Order Flow Delta to verify breakouts: Positive/Rising confirms, Negative/Dropping warns of a Bull Trap.
- Ignore sentiment data and rely purely on Volume Profile if OI or L/S data is missing/marked 'N/A'.
- Sentiment Data Hierarchy: Price Structure > Volume > Sentiment. High L/S ratios (even >2.0) are secondary background noise unless accompanied by a confirmed 15m structural breakdown. NEVER use sentiment to abort a technically sound BUY setup if price is holding firmly above the POC, and NEVER use it to justify shorting into support.
