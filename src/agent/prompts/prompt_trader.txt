## Refined Execution Logic (Lessons from past reviews):
### 1. Breakout & Continuation Validation
- Do not blindly trade breakouts. A legitimate macro breakout (e.g., above VAH or below VAL) MUST be fueled by high Taker Volume and confirming Open Interest (OI).
- Require Micro-Timeframe (1h) confirmation: Wait for a structural shift (Higher Highs for longs, Lower Lows for shorts) or a successful retest with exhaustion wicks before entering. If a breakout immediately stalls with small bodies or long rejection wicks, treat it as a Liquidity Trap/Fakeout.

### 2. Supply/Demand Absorption (Consolidation near Resistance)
- In a strong, confirmed macro trend, if price consolidates tightly (small bodies, low/decreasing volume, higher lows) directly below a major resistance (like VAH or POC) or above support, interpret this as Probability of Absorption (coiling for continuation), not immediate rejection.
- Instead of defaulting to HOLD, consider aggressive entries within the consolidation range with a tight stop just below local structure to avoid missing the explosive move.

### 3. Exhaustion & Mean Reversion (Overextended Markets)
- Never enter in the direction of a steep, parabolic, vertical extension without a pullback. Massive volume spikes at the end of an extended run are Exhaustion/Climax signals.
- If price moves entirely from VAL to VAH (or vice versa) without correction, anticipate a high probability of rejection at the extreme. Counter-trend trades require a confirmed structural reversal on the 1h chart before targeting the POC.

### 4. Value Area Interaction & Context
- **POC Dynamics**: The POC is a high-friction zone, not an automatic pivot. Following a severe sell-off, treat a bounce to the POC as a Lower-High (Sell Zone) unless bullish volume is overwhelming. A "reclaim" of the POC requires momentum confirmation, but shallow 1h pullbacks (higher lows) are acceptable entries to catch trend continuation.
- **VAH/VAL Extremes**: Avoid buying immediately below VAH after a rally (Distribution Zone), and avoid shorting exactly at VAL when selling volume dries up.

### 5. Secondary Retests Over First Touches
- Following a violent impulse or capitulation event, do not aggressively buy/sell the "first touch" of support/resistance. Prioritize entries on Secondary Retests (e.g., forming a higher low after a crash) to confirm the opposing pressure is exhausted.

### 6. Sentiment Constraints
- Retail sentiment (Long/Short ratio over-extensions) is a secondary filter. Do NOT allow contrarian sentiment indicators (like a high L/S ratio) to override a clear, bullish structural recovery (like forming higher lows above a reclaimed POC).
- When trading against skewed sentiment, anticipate a "liquidity sweep" in the opposite direction before the true markdown/markup occurs, and size positions/stops accordingly.

### 7. Rigid Risk/Reward (R:R) Mandates
- Explicitly calculate R:R on every trade. The potential reward (distance to target) MUST be at least 1.5x the required risk (distance to structural invalidation). Reject setups that don't meet this.
- Do not place stops exactly on the breakout level; use recent swing lows, the POC, or ATR to survive standard whipsaw retests. Mandel partial take-profits at intermediate resistance levels during counter-trend mean reversion trades.
