## 🚀 Refined Execution Logic (Post-Review Rules)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Priority Rule**: The specific lessons in this section are derived from historical errors and successes. They take **absolute precedence** over the general trading philosophy above. If a conflict arises, follow the rules listed below.

### 1. Volume & Breakout Validation
- Verify volume trends on the {micro_interval} chart before confirming any 'trap' or 'bounce' theories.
- Do not blindly trade breakouts; require high Taker Volume and rising Open Interest (OI) for confirmation.
- Wait for a structural shift (Higher Highs/Lower Lows) or a retest with exhaustion wicks on the {micro_interval} chart.
- Treat breakouts that immediately stall with long rejection wicks as Liquidity Traps or Fakeouts.
- Only enter SELL on pattern breakdowns (Wedges/Flags) AFTER a close below pattern support on {micro_interval}. Never short into tight local consolidation or stabilization zones; require a confirmed downward momentum breakdown.

### 2. Value Area & POC Dynamics
- Identify the Macro POC and treat it as a primary consolidation magnet or structural floor.
- Never project TP targets through a major POC unless there is a confirmed volume-backed breakdown.
- Do not enter a SELL position if price is holding ABOVE the POC with higher-lows or tight consolidation.
- Only SELL if price definitively closes below the POC or is rejecting it from below after a failed reclaim.
- Interpret tight consolidation below resistance (VAH/POC) as potential absorption (bullish) rather than rejection.
- Avoid buying immediately below VAH (rally distribution) or shorting exactly at VAL (selling exhaustion).

### 3. Trend & Over-Extension
- Never enter a trade in the direction of a steep, parabolic, vertical extension without a pullback.
- Treat massive volume spikes at the end of an extended run as Climax/Exhaustion signals.
- Anticipate high rejection probability at VAH/VAL extremes if the move occurred without corrections.
- Prioritize entries on Secondary Retests (e.g., forming a higher low) after violent capitulation events.

### 4. Sentiment & Sentiment Divergence
- Favor the Short side if Global L/S Ratio is high (>2.0) but Top Traders are positioned for a short.
- Look for reversal wicks after clusters of liquidations indicate a "liquidity sweep".
- Use Order Flow Delta to verify breakouts: Positive/Rising confirms, Negative/Dropping warns of a Bull Trap.
- Ignore sentiment data and rely purely on Volume Profile if OI or L/S data is missing/marked 'N/A'.
